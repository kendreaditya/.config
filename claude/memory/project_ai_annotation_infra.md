Emerging startup idea at the intersection of the user's Meta signals experience and the AI training data supply chain. The insight: current RLHF annotation (Scale AI, Mercor) captures only binary preference pairs (A > B) while discarding all implicit behavioral signals — response time, cursor trajectories, gaze patterns, reading patterns, confidence levels, reasoning traces. Meta spent billions learning that implicit signals predict behavior far better than stated preferences. Nobody has applied this to AI annotation.

**Why:** AI alignment is stuck because preference pairs are too thin. The "why" behind human judgment is lost. Richer signal capture could produce better reward models.

**How to apply:** The user's Meta signals expertise gives them unique founder-market fit to build an annotation platform that captures multi-dimensional implicit + explicit signals during RLHF tasks, producing dramatically richer training data for AI labs.

Connected to broader interests: rubric creation as forced quantification of expertise, knowledge infrastructure, the evolution from Scale (task-level) to Mercor (job-level) to deeper cognitive-level data capture.
