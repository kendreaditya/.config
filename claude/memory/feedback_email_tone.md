Keep emails casual and friendly, not overly professional.
Do not use dashes or em-dashes in email drafts.
Avoid "impressive" jargon phrases like "quote-to-cash workflow is a real problem" — just say it sounds interesting.
Use simple, direct language like "Would love to chat" over "I would be delighted to discuss."

**Why:** User explicitly corrected overly formal drafts and removed dashes. Preferred tone matches how they write naturally (short, friendly, no fluff).

**How to apply:** All email drafts for job outreach, recruiter replies, and LinkedIn messages. Always draft first and wait for "send" confirmation.
