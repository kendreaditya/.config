# Exemplars

Real emails from the 100-email corpus, covering common situations. Match length and register to the closest one when drafting.

## 1. Ultra-short reply (10 words)

```
Hi Ben, look interesting any JD's to send my way?
```

Situation: quick casual reply to a recruiter in an active thread. No signoff, no greeting line break.

## 2. Short warm reply (25 words)

```
Hi Christina,

Sorry for the late reply! Faction sounds interesting and would be open to a chat. What does the process look like?

Best,
Aditya
```

Situation: late reply to an inbound recruiter. One apology, one response, one question, signoff.

## 3. Delivery with numbered attachment list (34 words)

```
Hi Dawn,

Please find attached:

1. Separation Agreement
2. Severance Plan for Performance
3. Offer Letter
4. Benefits Upon Termination
5. Termination call transcript

Please let me know if you need anything else.

Aditya
```

Situation: sending documents to an attorney. Numbered list (not bulleted) when items are discrete documents. No "Best," — just `Aditya` for short formal sends.

## 4. Follow-up with factual recap (53 words)

```
Hi Dan,

Just following up on my email from March 25. I returned all company equipment (laptop, phone, badge, Meta Quest Pro + controllers) to MPK 14 and the team there confirmed they would store everything in the locker.

Could you please confirm receipt on your end when you get a chance?

Situation: nudging HR for confirmation. Opens with `Just following up`, restates the key fact with specifics, ends with a direct ask.

## 5. Cold ask with quick summary (76 words)

```

Quick summary: former Meta E4 engineer, terminated for performance, 12 weeks base salary offer. I have documentary evidence of manager misconduct including backdated growth plan entries and factual inaccuracies in the written rationale.

Aditya

Situation: cold outreach to a potential attorney. `Quick summary:` header is a signature pattern. Specific ask at the end.

## 7. Cold outreach / job application (169 words)

```
Hi Phylo team,

I came across Phylo through your newsletter and wanted to reach out about the Member of Technical Staff, Product Engineering role.

I'm a software engineer coming from Meta where I worked on full stack product features for the ads recommendation platform (React, TypeScript, A/B testing at scale). Before that I built an AI agent chat product from scratch at Kimberly Clark serving 45,000 users, complete with auth, permissions, and chat history. I've also shipped a cross platform music app with React Native and Next.js to 10K users.

What got me excited about Phylo is the intersection of AI agents and actual scientific research. The Biomni Lab preview users accomplishing 20 months of work in one is wild, and turning those AI capabilities into intuitive product experiences for researchers is exactly the kind of work I want to be doing. I also have a biomedical research background (published in IEEE ISBI, ISEF finalist) so the domain genuinely interests me.

Resume attached. Would love to chat.

Best,
Aditya
```

Situation: cold job inquiry. Three-paragraph structure — hook, credentials with concrete metrics, why *them* specifically with evidence they've read the company's material. `Resume attached. Would love to chat.` is the standard close.

## 8. Structured incident report (226 words)

```
Hi Drew,

Thanks for setting up the claim. Here's everything I have from the accident.

What happened: I was in a Target parking lot in Santa Clara on March 31 around 6:10 PM. I was stopped in the middle lane, waiting for pedestrians to pass before reversing into a parking spot, when the other driver (Ernesto Rivas Aguilar) pulled out of his parking spot and reversed his 2008 Honda CR-V into the left rear quarter panel of my car. No injuries.

Important: Ernesto's insurance (AAA/CSAA) and vehicle registration are both expired. His driver's license is the only valid document he had. All the details are in the report below.

A few things to note:
- The vehicle that was hit is a rental (2025 Hyundai Elantra from Enterprise, RA# 2KXTG8). My VW ID.4 is currently in the shop from a prior claim (#8851529940000001).
- I plan to contact Target (Santa Clara) to request they preserve the parking lot security footage from that evening.
- I have photos of all of Ernesto's documents (license, expired insurance card, expired registration), photos of the damage, and videos from the scene.

Links:
- Full accident report: https://docs.google.com/document/d/128DukGTbo4dqQfSonfhzTGTEjAInN8AxjHwCPAXs3wc/edit
- Photos and videos from the scene: https://photos.app.goo.gl/7GSmpsqjyGBtDuQh7

Also, since his insurance is expired, will you be pursuing subrogation to recover my deductible? Is there anything else you need from me?

Thanks,
Aditya
```

Situation: providing the full facts of an incident to an insurance adjuster. Labeled sections (`What happened:`, `Important:`, `A few things to note:`, `Links:`) with bullets where helpful. Closes with two specific questions. `Thanks,` signoff (not `Best,`) because it's acknowledging their work.

## 9. Detailed status summary with tables (304 words)

```
Hi Matt,

Just submitted the payment. Here's a summary to get you up to speed.

Timeline:
- March 11, 2026 — Terminated for performance, system access revoked
- April 10, 2026 — Official separation date
- April 17, 2026 at 2:00 PM PST — Signing deadline (5 business days after separation)

The Offer:
- $40,721.28 cash (12 weeks base salary, lump sum)
- 6 months COBRA (Meta pays 100%)
- 3 months career outplacement via LHH

Key Context:
- Meta's standard layoff formula is 16 weeks + 2 weeks/year of service. My offer of 12 weeks under the "performance" plan is roughly $20K below what laid-off employees received.
- My base salary was $173,000/year. I was an E4 Software Engineer on the Dynamic Ads / Monetization team for about 1 year.

Evidence:
- A timestamped copy of my growth plan downloaded December 12, 2025 showing the feedback section was completely blank.
- No negative feedback was communicated before either performance review cycle. My manager gave consistently positive signals throughout ("you are doing great," "no red flag," "you're in good shape").
- My manager set conditions that prevented a project from launching, then cited the non-launch as the primary basis for the negative rating.

All key documents are attached. Let me know if you need anything else or what the next steps are.

Thanks,
Aditya
```

Situation: onboarding a new attorney. Multiple labeled sections, em-dashes separating date from description, dollar amounts exact. Closing is the canonical "Let me know if you need anything else or what the next steps are."

## Register-matching cheatsheet

| Situation | Length | Signoff |
|---|---|---|
| Active casual thread | 1 line, maybe no greeting break | (none) or `Aditya` |
| Quick reply to recruiter/peer | 20-50 words | `Best,\nAditya` |
| Thanks-for-help acknowledgment | 30-80 words | `Thanks,\nAditya` |
| Cold outreach, formal ask | 60-180 words | `Best,\nAditya` (or `Thank you,\nAditya` to attorneys/professionals) |
| Structured report / summary | 200-400 words | `Thanks,\nAditya` |
| Legal/contractual | any | `Aditya Kendre` (full name) |
